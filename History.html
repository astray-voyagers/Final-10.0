History In Video Games

From Start to Now

1972 – 1973

Pong was created by Atari as the first commercialize video games. It was a simulation of real-life table tennis was marketed as an arcade game. The game was available in limited copies in late 1972 and started to reach the market in large quantities in March 1973.

1974 – 1976

Due to the drop in the sale of Pong-like games, games company started to produce games in different genre. Some of these are Gran Trak 10 (1974) and Tank (1974) from Atari and gun Fight (1975) Seawolf (1976) from Midway. Gun Fight was also the first game to incorporated microprocessor thus starting the era of video games programmed in software instead of hardware.

1976 – 1978

The company, Magnavox odyssey manages to reduce the part count of the microprocessor into three-chip set thus releasing the Odyssey 100 and Odyssey 200. Atari in the meanwhile take it a step even further by producing the single-chip which contain Home-Pong. By the year 1977, a new wave of programmable system hit the market which contain games that are stored in cartridges. The cartridges can be plugged directly into the CPU of the console. These changes caused many companies that are unable to adapt to exit the industry.

1978 – 1982

From the success of Breakout (1977), Tomohiro Nishikado created the game Space invader by replacing the paddle with gun battery and the bricks with rows of aliens. The game was released in 1978 and receive a huge success. It also introduced many new concepts that become prominent in arcade games such as such as lives-regulated play and highest-score-tracking. It was also the first game to include a background music which consist of a four-note loop. The success then sparks the birth of other games such as Pac-Man, Defender and Q*bert.

1980 – 1982

In 1980, Space Invaders was released for the VCS by Atari. This had become a breakthrough for home video game market. Atari then proceed step up their game even further by also converting other hit games such as Missile Command and Asteroids. This not only place them as the commanding lead in the industry but also manage to take Fairchild out of the competition. In 1979, four Atari programmers form a new company called Activision which manage to produce a streak of hit including Kaboom! (1982) and River Raid (1982). Atari the tried to compete with its rival by releasing Atari 5200 but it failed to perform as good as its predecessor. In 1980, Nintendo released a handheld electronic game called Game & Watch which spur dozens of other portable games.

1983 – 1995

NES was released in 1985 by Nintendo in US which succeed to revive the gaming industries after its downfall in late 1983. It was redesigned of Famicom which was released in Japan. Soon it managed to become the great console of the third generation along with the Master System. In this generation, gamepad took over from the traditional joystick as the default game controller. Many high-profile role-playing franchises such as The Legend of Zelda and Metal gear was released. The generation ended when NES was discontinued in 1995.

1987 – 2004

The 16-bit generation consoles started to emerge during these periods. In 1988 Sega release Mega Drive and not long after, Nintendo released SNES in 1990. CD-ROM drives were introduced in this generation which sparks the starting of PlayStation from Sony. Handheld console was also revived in these eras as the Nintendo’s Game Boy (1989), which became widely popular. The success of Game Boy was attributed to the best-selling titles Pokémon Red and Blue.

1993 – 2005

Atari Jaguar was released in 1993 by Atari, marking its re-entry into the home console market. 3DO Interactive multiplayer was also released by 3DO Company in the same year though it failed to compete with Jaguar due to its high price. However, both consoles had a very low sales and few quality games. In 1994, Sega Saturn, PlayStation and PC-FX was released. PlayStation quickly outsold all its competitor except the SNES due to SNES’s large reservoir of support from game companies. 1995 marked the release of Virtual Boy from Nintendo marking the first consoles providing 3D depth perception. It does not receive high sales due to the monochromatic display and lack of third-party support. Nintendo-64 release in 1996 was defined by the game Super Mario 64, despite the console itself never really gained any revenue due to its usage of ROM cartridges instead of CD-ROMs. Many game companies prefer using CD-ROMs thus resulting in Nintendo getting left behind. By 2005, Sony had become the leading company in the market.

1998 – 2013

This period marks the release of sixth generation consoles such as Sega Dreamcast, PlayStation 2, GameCube and Xbox. With the exception of Dreamcast, all of these consoles receive positive review. In 2000, the release of Game Boy Advance placed Nintendo as the dominating company in term of handheld console. The generation ended after the discontinuation of PlayStation 2.

2005 – present

The seventh generation debuted when PlayStation Portable (PSP) Nintendo DS (NDS) debuted in 2004. PSP favour graphic and power while NDS favour low-power design but having novel control interface, primarily its dual screen interface which makes its defining feature. In console gaming, Xbox 360 was released by Microsoft, followed by PlayStation 3 Sony. The two consoles had a heated rivalry. Nintendo comes to lead the market with the release of Wii, and it is currently Nintendo’s bestselling home console.

2011 – present

In 2011, N3DS was released marking the start of eight generation consoles. This is followed by the PS Vita in the same year. Wii U was released in 2012 as the successor of Wii. PS4 was released in the following year, deemed by Sony as the successor of PS3. To compete with these outstanding consoles, Microsoft released Xbox one as successor of Xbox 360. Due to Wii U slow consumer adoption, it was ended in 2017 and Nintendo release the new Nintendo Switch. Wii U was noted for pioneering several concepts that were implemented and refined in Nintendo Switch. Switch managed to competitively compete with PS4 and Xbox One.
